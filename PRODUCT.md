# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Young African leaders (students, first-class graduates, early-career changemakers) exploring recognition and opportunity; partner organisations, sponsors, and speakers evaluating collaboration; existing Top100 awardees returning for events, the member hub, and the magazine.

## Product Purpose

Top100 Africa Future Leaders identifies, empowers, and celebrates Africa's youth leaders across 31 countries. The site is the movement's front door: awardee directory and profiles, annual summit and festival, magazine editions, blog, events hub, and application/partnership flows, plus an authenticated member hub and admin console.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious, warm, credible. Celebratory without being loud; institutional enough for partners like One Young World, ALX, and the Learning Planet Institute, yet youthful and energetic for the awardee audience.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing-page template (hero metric rows, identical icon-card grids).
- Corporate NGO grayness — the brand is saturated orange/amber, not muted slate.
- Dark "web3 conference" aesthetics with neon gradients.

## Design Principles

1. **Celebrate people first** — photography of real awardees, speakers, and gatherings carries the story.
2. **Orange is the voice** — the committed orange→amber gradient identifies every primary action and the header; neutrals stay warm (white → #fffaf4 → #f7f3ec).
3. **Soft, generous geometry** — large radii (rounded-[22px]–[32px]), orange-100 hairline borders, minimal shadows.
4. **Dark panels for gravitas** — near-black (#05060f) rounded heroes and CTA bands with subtle radial/conic color washes mark the big moments.
5. **Clarity over cleverness** — plain-language copy, direct CTAs (Apply, Partner, Contact us).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast (slate-600+ on warm whites, white/72+ on dark panels), visible focus rings (focus-visible + orange ring pattern in the header), semantic headings, alt text on imagery, reduced-motion respected for decorative animation.
